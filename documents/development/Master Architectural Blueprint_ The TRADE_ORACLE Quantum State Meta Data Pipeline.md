## **3\. Metadata Collection: The 4,380-Cycle Infrastructure**

The objective of the six-month forward-testing phase is to aggregate exactly 4,380 execution cycles (amounting to one hourly heartbeat evaluation per day across the timeframe). This data serves as the critical high-dimensional precursor for quantum machine learning and combinatorial optimization.2  
A "cycle" within the TRADE\_ORACLE system is defined as a complete traversal of the quantitative scanner, the LangGraph Super Brain evaluation, risk compliance verification, and post-trade execution forensics.2 To ensure the data maintains the fidelity required for Quantum Unconstrained Binary Optimization (QUBO) translation, the system structures the collected metadata across five distinct, isolated layers.2

### **3.1 Layer 1: The Quantitative DNA**

The foundational layer captures the explicit mathematical state of the target asset and the benchmark asset (Bitcoin) at the precise moment of algorithmic evaluation.2 This layer is designed to identify technical clusters and non-linear market geometry. The encoded fields include the market\_regime flag, categorized as bullish or bearish based on the asset's relative positioning to the 50-day Simple Moving Average (SMA).\[2, 2\] It records the btc\_proximity\_to\_50sma, measuring the percentage distance of the benchmark from systemic support, alongside the asset\_rel\_strength\_index, which calculates the ratio of the target asset price against the benchmark price at entry.2 Furthermore, the confluence\_indicators object captures the precise percentage distance from the daily 20-EMA (ema\_20\_daily\_dist) and the tagged Fibonacci retracement levels.2 Crucially, the raw 14-day Average True Range (volatility\_atr\_14) is logged as the primary metric for dynamic stop-loss derivation and subsequent risk parity calculations.\[2, 2\]

### **3.2 Layer 2: The AI Super-Brain Synthesis**

The second layer bridges classical mathematics with semantic artificial intelligence reasoning, capturing the qualitative outputs of the LangGraph topology.2 This includes the model identifier (e.g., GPT-5.5, DeepSeek-V3.2, or Grok 4.20) to track longitudinal reasoning performance across different commercial models.1  
The critical output is the ai\_conviction\_score, a normalized float rating from 1.0 to 10.0 that quantifies the AI's confidence in the setup based on the synthesis of the specialist nodes.2 Additionally, the narrative\_tags array logs the specific fundamental catalysts driving the logic, such as "AI-Agents", "Supply-Unlock", or "Macro-Hawkish".2 A compliance\_proof\_hash stores a highly compressed snippet of the LLM’s explanation, ensuring complete auditability of the decision-making process against proprietary trading constraints.2 Inference latency in milliseconds is also recorded to optimize the orchestration timing.2

### **3.3 Layer 3: Risk and Institutional Guardrails**

The third layer records the immutable state invariants related to the account's equity and the strict constraints governing execution.2 It logs the equity\_high\_watermark at the exact moment of evaluation, tracking the absolute peak portfolio value required to calculate the trailing drawdown limit.\[2, 2\] The current\_drawdown\_pct measures the exact distance from this high watermark.2 The concurrency\_count logs the number of other active trades (ranging from zero to the hard cap of four), and the risk\_per\_trade\_usd logs the enforced $10.00 parameter.2 Finally, the calculated\_lot\_size records the exact execution volume after normalizing for the target asset's volatility.2

### **3.4 Layer 4 and 5: Execution Forensics and Post-Trade Wisdom**

The final two layers capture the physical friction of market interaction and the ultimate outcome of the algorithmic decision.2 Layer 4 records whether the order was transmitted as a LIMIT or MARKET execution, comparing the requested\_entry\_price against the actual fill\_price to mathematically derive real-world slippage parameters.2 It also captures execution\_latency\_ms, tracking the delay from the LangGraph gatekeeper approval to the Match-Trader API fill, alongside exact broker commissions and holding swap rates.2  
Layer 5, the Post-Trade Audit, is the most vital component for downstream quantum optimization. It records the Maximum Adverse Excursion (mae), measuring the furthest distance the asset traveled against the position, and the Maximum Favorable Excursion (mfe), measuring the ultimate profit potential of the setup.2 The final exit\_reason (e.g., TP1\_HIT, SL\_HIT, or MANUAL\_CLOSE) provides the deterministic conclusion to the 4,380-cycle sequence.2

## **6\. Processing Pipeline: QPU Integration via AWS Braket**

Feeding the accumulated 4,380 metadata cycles into a physical quantum processing unit presents severe architectural and latency challenges. Physical QPUs—such as the D-Wave Advantage2 or the trapped-ion systems like IonQ Forte—are highly scarce, expensive, and subject to significant public queuing delays.3 It is fundamentally impossible and latency-prohibitive to process every live intraday setup directly on quantum hardware.2  
Consequently, the TRADE\_ORACLE architecture utilizes the QPU not as a real-time execution switch, but as an asynchronous Macro-Global Optimizer.2 The QPU is engaged in batch processing intervals to globally optimize the portfolio weights and hyperparameter landscapes, which are then injected back into the local Python daemon for rapid execution.2 To construct this pipeline flawlessly by the 2026-2027 horizon, the system relies exclusively on Amazon Braket Hybrid Jobs.28

### **6.1 AWS Braket Hybrid Jobs Execution Flow**

Amazon Braket Hybrid Jobs provides a fully managed orchestration environment specifically engineered to bridge classical AWS compute resources with quantum hardware access.28 This infrastructure is ideal for iterative algorithms like the Quantum Approximate Optimization Algorithm (QAOA) that require continuous, low-latency feedback loops between the CPU and the QPU.28  
The end-to-end processing pipeline operates through the following highly orchestrated sequence:

1. **Data Extraction and Staging:** Upon triggering a batch optimization sequence, the Python daemon extracts the high-dimensional vectors and historical state data from Milvus and Supabase. The system formats the data into a standard dictionary (input\_data) and uploads it to an Amazon S3 bucket. This triggers the AMZN\_BRAKET\_INPUT\_DIR environment variable within the Braket container.33  
2. **Containerized Pre-processing:** The execution logic utilizes the @hybrid\_job Python decorator provided by the Amazon Braket SDK.31 This annotation automatically provisions a scalable Amazon EC2 instance tailored to the classical memory requirements of the task.28  
3. **Mathematical Translation (dimod):** Within the secure EC2 container, the classical CPU utilizes the D-Wave Ocean SDK's dimod library to finalize the QUBO ![][image20]\-matrix, executing the binary expansion algorithms and structuring the exact linear and quadratic coefficients derived from the LLM conviction scores and ATR matrices.2  
4. **Priority QPU Submission:** The fully structured ![][image20]\-matrix is submitted from the EC2 instance directly to the target QPU (e.g., the D-Wave Advantage2).2 A critical architectural advantage of utilizing Braket Hybrid Jobs is that associated quantum tasks receive priority queueing, bypassing standard public wait times and ensuring the algorithm is not interrupted by external queue fluctuations.28  
5. **Quantum Annealing Execution:** The D-Wave hardware executes the physical annealing cycle. Utilizing over 4,400 active qubits and advanced Fast Anneal topology, the system simultaneously explores the multidimensional BQM energy landscape.6 By leveraging quantum tunneling to bypass local minima, the hardware identifies the global minimum energy state, which perfectly represents the optimal risk-adjusted allocation parameters.1  
6. **Parameter Injection and State Retrieval:** The optimized multi-asset weights are returned to the EC2 container, which writes the outputs back to the designated S3 bucket before automatically releasing the classical compute instances to minimize cloud expenditure.28 The local Python daemon retrieves the optimal weights and dynamically updates the configurations of the LangGraph Super Brain, completing the feedback loop.2

## **7\. Strategy Tuning: Modulating Classical Logic via Quantum Metadata**

The explicit objective of collecting exactly 4,380 cycles of high-fidelity quantum state metadata is to directly tune and refine the current trading strategy after the initial burn-in period. Legacy quantitative optimization faces severe computational roadblocks when attempting to identify the optimal configuration of parameters across dynamic market regimes.2  
The legacy tuning framework of the TRADE\_ORACLE system relied heavily on multidimensional parameter sweeps utilizing the vectorbt library.2 This classical approach involved converting price series and indicators into massive arrays, utilizing NumPy and Numba-compiled operations to sweep hundreds of thousands of permutations simultaneously.2 The objective was to discover the mathematical sweet spot that maximized trade frequency while preserving the integrity of the setup constraints.2 Variables included sweeping the macroeconomic regime filter (from 20-day to 50-day SMAs), expanding momentum velocity thresholds (evaluating 2%, 3%, 4%, and 5% standard deviations over varying windows), and analyzing precise proximity bands (1% to 4%) around key Fibonacci convergence zones.2  
While vectorbt solves the computational speed problem for isolated variables, it is entirely inadequate for holistic multidimensional tuning.2 As the parameter space expands, classical grid search succumbs to the curse of dimensionality; evaluating every permutation of every variable scales exponentially, resulting in intractable processing times and severe memory bottlenecks.2 Furthermore, classical parameter sweeps fail to account for chronological path dependencies.2 The system was forced to pass the optimized outputs through a secondary engine, Backtrader, which simulates the chronological reality of equity-based trailing drawdowns to verify if a strategy survived the draconian $150 Maven Trading limit.2 Separating volume optimization from survival stress-testing is inherently inefficient.

### **7.1 Quantum Grid Search and Stochastic Quantum Tuning**

To transcend these classical limitations, the Quantum State Meta Data pipeline employs advanced **Stochastic Quantum Tuning** via **Quantum Grid Search**.2 Rather than utilizing the QPU exclusively for real-time asset selection, the system reformulates the *strategy hyperparameters themselves* as the primary decision variables within the QUBO framework.  
The back-tuning methodology is executed precisely as follows:

1. **Binary Autoencoder (bAE) Compression:** The vast hyperparameter search space (comprising continuous thresholds, discrete SMA lengths, and fractional scaling modifiers) is deeply compressed into a binary latent space.19 A binary autoencoder is trained on the classical feasible solutions generated during the 4,380 cycles. This ensures that the binary encoding accurately reflects the true neighborhood structure of the data, so that a small Hamming bit-flip during quantum annealing corresponds to a logical, meaningful modification in the trading strategy rather than generating invalid parameter constraints.19  
2. **Surrogate Model Construction (FMQA):** Because the direct trading simulation (calculating the path-dependent $150 trailing drawdown) is a non-linear "black box" function, it cannot be directly embedded into an Ising Hamiltonian.19 Therefore, the system utilizes Factorization Machine with Quantum Annealing (FMQA).19 FMQA builds a quadratic surrogate model based on the 4,380 cycles of execution forensics (Layer 4\) and Post-Trade audit data (Layer 5\) stored in the Quantum Vault.2  
3. **Drawdown Energy Minimization:** The surrogate objective function is strictly parameterized to minimize "Drawdown Energy" while maximizing the correlation between the LLM ai\_conviction\_score and actual Maximum Favorable Excursion (mfe).\[2, 2\] Parameter combinations that historically resulted in severe Maximum Adverse Excursions (mae) or repeatedly threatened the 3% Maven Trading limit are assigned massive energy penalties.\[2, 2, 37\]  
4. **Annealing the Hyperparameters:** The D-Wave QPU solves the FMQA surrogate model, bypassing local optima to find the absolute global minimum of the parameter space.19 The output provides the precise, mathematically verified combination of settings—for example, dictating that under a high-inflation macro regime, the system must utilize a 38.2% Fibonacci band, a 34-day SMA filter, and an exact 0.025 momentum velocity threshold to achieve optimal risk-adjusted yield.  
5. **The Iterative Recalibration Loop:** Stochastic Quantum Tuning is not a static backtest; it is an active feedback mechanism. Following the initial 4,380-cycle optimization, the system executes a Quantum Refactor every subsequent 500 cycles.2 The actual post-trade outcomes stored in Supabase are continuously cross-referenced against the LangGraph Super Brain's theoretical conviction predictions.\[2, 2\] The differences act as a gradient to continually adjust the linear coefficients (![][image21]) of the BQM, allowing the trading parameters to organically evolve in tandem with shifting global liquidity dynamics.

### **7.2 Counterfactual "What-If" Scenario Generation**

Crucially, the tuning process utilizes the 4,380 cycles of quantum state metadata to construct counterfactual "what-if" scenarios. By evaluating the historical state data housed in the Quantum Vault, the system can systematically simulate how the current strategy *would have* behaved if different LLM conviction thresholds, moving average lengths, or momentum variables had been manually changed during past market conditions.  
During optimization, these what-if scenarios are leveraged to generate alternate QUBO solutions under varying penalty configurations. This highly specialized capability allows the QPU to test alternative parameter combinations against historical execution metadata without risking live capital. It inherently validates that the refined strategy parameters, once injected back into the Python daemon, are mathematically robust against past liquidity dynamics and structural market regime shifts.
